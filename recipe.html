Chilaquiles

Chucks personal favorite for a quick breakfast. This recipe features crispy tortilla chips and Chuck's homemade salsa that's to boost the flavor.

Ingredients:
  • Chucks Salsa
  • Fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 bag of tortilla chips
  • 4 eggs
  • 1/2 cup crumbled queso fresco
Instructions:
  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Spread a layer of tortilla chips on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Spoon a layer of the Chucks homemade salsa over the tortilla chips.
  3. Repeat layers until all the tortilla chips and salsa are used up.
  4. Crack the eggs on top of the salsa and bake in the oven for 20-25 minutes or until the eggs are cooked to your liking.
  5. Sprinkle crumbled queso fresco on top and garnish with more cilantro before serving.
Ingredients:
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cooked and shredded
  • 2 cups Chuck's Homemade Salsa
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped green bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup chopped red bell pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 10-12 corn tortillas
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
Instructions:
  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the shredded chicken, Chuck's Homemade Salsa, onion, bell peppers, garlic, cumin, chili powder, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Warm tortillas in the microwave or on a griddle until pliable.
  4. Spoon the chicken mixture onto each tortilla and roll up tightly. Place the enchiladas seam side down in a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  5. Pour any remaining salsa mixture over the enchiladas and sprinkle the shredded cheese on top.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until heated through and cheese is melted and bubbly. Garnish with cilantro before serving.

Ceviche
This ceviche recipe is easy to make and bursting with flavor, thanks to the addition of Chuck's Salsa. This is a refreshing dish that's perfect for a warm summer day.
Ingredients:
  • 1 pound of fresh white fish (such as snapper, halibut, or cod), cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1/2 cup Chuck's Salsa
  • 1/2 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Tortilla chips, for serving
Instructions:
  1. In a medium bowl, combine the fish, lime juice, and Chuck's Salsa. Mix well to ensure that all of the fish is coated in the lime juice and salsa. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for 15-20 minutes to allow the fish to "cook" in the lime juice.
  2. While the fish is marinating, chop the red onion, jalapeño pepper, and cilantro leaves. Set aside.
  3. After 15-20 minutes, remove the fish from the refrigerator and stir in the chopped red onion, jalapeño pepper, and cilantro leaves. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Serve the ceviche cold, topped with diced avocado and more cilantro leaves, if desired. Enjoy with tortilla chips and a cold beer!
